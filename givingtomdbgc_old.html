Giving to MDBGC

About
The Mathews-Dickey Boys' & Girls' Club comes from humble beginnings, founded under a shade tree in 1960 by two neighborhood baseball coaches, Martin Luther Mathews and the late Hubert "Dickey" Ballentine. From a small storefront of 30 boys the Club has grown to serve more than 40,000 young men and women, ages 5-18, in a 50-mile radius throughout the St. Louis-metropolitan area. The Club also acts as a meeting hub for various community, social and business organizations and as an outreach resource center for youth from all walks of life and socio-economic backgrounds.

The Need
The Club is a non-profit, 501(c)(3) United Way member agency and relies on the generosity of individuals just like you to fund a wholesome array of cultural, educational and sports programs for young people. As the needs of our community continue to grow, so does the financial resources required to support and sustain our programs well into the future.  You can make a difference.

Our Mission
Initially, the Club was designed to provide structured recreational activities to neighborhood youths. Today, the Club's goals are more focused and comprehensive and are aimed at:

  • Emphasizing the three Rs (Respect, Restraint and Responsibility) through athletic competition and team-building.
  • Building self-esteem, while allowing for personal growth, emphasizing the importance of becoming literate and educated citizens.
  • Addressing problems stemming from teen idleness.
  • Instilling the desire to lead productive lives and to become positive contributors and future leaders in the community.
  • Teaching moral, personal, social, cultural development and health & wellness practices.

Giving to Mathews-Dickey

Introduction

Mathews-Dickey Boys' & Girls' Club has been a pillar of the St. Louis community for more than half a century. Since then, 2 million-plus young men and women have relied on the Club as a home-away-from-home to learn, play, prosper and grow on a daily basis.  Mathews-Dickey relies on donations and other charitable gifts to keep these programs alive and well. 

How Your Giving Helps

Your giving to the Mathews-Dickey Boys' & Girls' Club goes directly to the support of one of our many wholesome and comprehensive programs.

  • Cultural & Health Awareness - Culinary arts and nutrition, dance and music.
  • Educational Enrichment & Leadership - After-school Computer technology, "Maleness to Manhood Workshop," "The Sky is the Limit," weekend volunteer tutorial and outreach (ACT preparation, college, health education and life skills workshops).
  • Sports - Baseball, basketball, Bulldog Football, cheerleading, Earn & Learn umpires and referees, lacrosse, swimming and tennis.
  • Summer Day Camp - Arts & crafts, educational and cultural field trips, group leader training, recreational activities and storytelling.

These programs teachyouth respect, restraint and responsibility so that they may obtain credibility, integrity and accountability, demonstrating that academics and athletics can and should co-exist.

Funds also can be earmarked to support the Martin Luther Mathews Legacy Endowment Fund, created in honor of the cofounder's retirement in 2014 as a mechanism to sustain the organization for generations of children to come.  The Club's Planned Giving Council is a knowledgeable volunteer group comprised of estate and tax-planning accountants and attorneys, investment planning consultants, banking experts and trust officers and/or life insurance agents.
