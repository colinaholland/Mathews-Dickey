The Mathews-Dickey Friends Club

When you join the Mathews-Dickey Friends Club you are helping the Club educate youth on the front end to keep them from falling through the cracks on the back end through leadership and career training experiences; build strong bodies and minds and explore their artistic and cultural aspirations.

Cultural Arts & Health

Culinary arts and nutrition, dance, martial arts, piano, urban music, violin and voice

Educational Enrichment

Computer technology, “Maleness to Manhood Workshop,” “The Sky is the Limit,” volunteer tutorial and outreach (ACT preparation, college, health education and life skills workshops)

Sports & Recreation

Baseball, basketball, Bulldog Football, cheerleading, Earn & Learn umpires, referees and group leaders, lacrosse, street hockey, swimming and tennis

Summer Day Camp

A home-away-from-home for youth while their parents work

The feeling of success and accomplishment is one that every child should experience and enjoy. As a Friend you are helping Mathews-Dickey give St. Louis area children the opportunity to ace a spelling test, score a touchdown, hit a homerun or land a free throw, value high morals and go above-and-beyond to realize success.

Mathews-Dickey is a juggernaut in galvanizing community support for a large volume of St. Louis area youth from all walks of life, ethnic and socio-economic backgrounds. This important mission has offered increased demand and responsibility to reach beyond traditional funding sources to capture new streams of support for innovative programs in cultural & health awareness, educational enrichment & leadership, sports & recreation and summer day camp.

More than 1 million young men and women from a 50-mile radius have participated in our activities since our Kingshighway building was erected and declared a model for the country by President Ronald Regan in 1982. The Club can only continue this mission if friends like you step forward. At this critical time we need your help more than ever to encourage our youth to give respect to gain respect, use restraint regardless of the situation and to be responsible to themselves, their communities and their country.

Mathews-Dickey Boys' & Girls' Club is a nonprofit 501 (c) (3) United Way member agency that annually provides educational, athletic and cultural enrichment programs to more than 40,000 young people from throughout the St. Louis metropolitan area. Since 1960 the Club has taught youth respect, restraint and responsibility so that they may obtain credibility, integrity and accountability. For more information, call Barbara A. Washington or Bill Fronczak at (314) 382-5952, ext. 234.
