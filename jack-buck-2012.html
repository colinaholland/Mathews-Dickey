Mathews-Dickey "Jack Buck Celebrity Night with the Stars" Celebrates 21st Anniversary


Andy Benes & Rick Horton



Kerry Robinson

VIEW MORE IMAGES IN
OUR PHOTO GALLERY

The Mathews-Dickey Boys’ & Girls’ Club celebrated an all-star evening at the 21st Annual “Jack Buck Celebrity Night with the Stars” on July 22 at the Missouri Athletic Club.  The high-level dinner auction generated more $45,000 for the Club’s newest venture: the Mathews-Dickey at Bob Russell Park.  The funds will be used to revitalize the field of dreams where youth can enjoy baseball, football, softball and Earn & Learn opportunities.

KMOX Radio’s Tom Ackerman emceed the live broadcast that evening and KTVI Fox 2’s Andy Banker served as the auctioneer.  Cardinals Broadcaster and event chair Rick Horton led a conversation with Cardinals greats including Andy Benes, Jack Clark and Kerry Robinson. 

The Mathews-Dickey Board Team Manager Jim Binz, managing director of The PrivateBank, recognized fellow directors who formed a starting line-up of table sponsors.  St. Louis Fire Chief Dennis Jenkerson added to the proceeds by announcing their $9,459.67 “Safety Weekend for Kids” Toll Road results.

Mathews-Dickey Vice President of Public Relations & Special Events Barbara A. Washington provided reflections on the event’s purpose and memory of Jack Buck.  President, CEO & Co-Founder Martin Luther Mathews applauded the board and offered acknowledgments to major sponsors Ameren Missouri, Edward Jones, KPMG and The PrivateBank.

Mathews-Dickey Boys' & Girls' Club is a nonprofit 501 (c) (3) United Way member agency that annually provides educational, athletic and cultural enrichment programs to more than 40,000 young people from throughout the St. Louis metropolitan area. For 52 years the Club has taught youth respect, restraint and responsibility so that they may obtain credibility, integrity and accountability. For more information, call Barbara A.
